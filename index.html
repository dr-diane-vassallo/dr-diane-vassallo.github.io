Dr. Diane Vassallo

B.Ed.(Hons)(Melit.) · M.Sc.(Lancs.) · Ph.D.(Belfast)

Lecturer & Researcher · Department of Technology and Entrepreneurship Education
Faculty of Education, University of Malta

Dr. Diane Vassallo is a Lecturer in the Department of Technology and Entrepreneurship Education at the Faculty of Education, University of Malta, where she teaches across undergraduate and postgraduate programmes in computing education and early childhood education and care.

Her research sits at the intersection of Computational Thinking, Executive Functions, and Artificial Intelligence in Education — exploring how digital tools and pedagogies can enhance learning across formal and informal contexts, and how emerging technologies such as AI can be integrated ethically and meaningfully into classrooms. She has a particular interest in learner agency, teacher professional development, and the cognitive foundations of computing education.

Dr. Vassallo read for her B.Ed. (Hons.) at the University of Malta, completed an M.Sc. in Learning Technologies at Lancaster University, and pursued her doctoral studies at Queen's University Belfast, where her research focused on mainstream technologies for students with learning difficulties. She is co-founder of Computational Thinking Malta and co-organiser of the Bebras Challenge in Malta.

ALPER-EU ↗

K12 Agile Learning of Programming with Educational Robotics. A European cooperation project bringing together policy makers, universities, and primary schools across five EU countries to strengthen early programming and robotics education through an agile, scrum-style learning approach. Develops a standardised, competence-based curriculum built around 60 gamified robotics "sprints", an engagement assessment tool, and an asynchronous teacher-training platform. Piloted in real classrooms including learners with ADHD, with EU-wide dissemination via the SCIENTIX network across 51 countries.

Period: December 2025 – November 2027  ·  Budget: €400,000

Academic Teaching

Teaching

I teach across undergraduate and postgraduate programmes at the Faculty of Education, University of Malta. My teaching bridges theory and practice in computing education, digital pedagogies, and early childhood education and care.

TEM5016  Introduction to Computational Thinking

Foundations of computational thinking — decomposition, pattern recognition, abstraction, and algorithms — explored through practical and unplugged activities.

TEM5007  Resources for Teaching Computing

Design, evaluation, and use of tools and resources for effective computing education, including physical computing, game-based learning, and digital creation.

TEM5013  Practice of Computing Education — Part 1

An introduction to the theory and practice of teaching computing at secondary level, grounded in current pedagogical research and curriculum frameworks.

TEM5015  Practice of Computing Education — Part 2

A continuation developing deeper competence in computing pedagogy, lesson design, and reflective professional practice in school contexts.

TEM5014  Assessment in Computing Education

Principles and practices of formative and summative assessment within computing education, including design of assessment tools aligned to curriculum outcomes.

TEM5018  Robots, Programmable Toys & Physical Computing

Hands-on exploration of robotics, programmable toys, and physical computing in educational settings, with a focus on early years and primary contexts.

LLI5025  Digital Pedagogies: A Hands-On Approach

Practical engagement with digital tools and pedagogies for diverse learning contexts, linking theory to classroom application across age groups.

ICT5101  Internet of Things

Conceptual and practical introduction to the Internet of Things — connected devices, data, and the implications of networked systems for society and education.

LLI5001 / LLI5002  Field Placement 1 & 2

Supervised school-based placements providing student teachers with structured, mentored experience in real classroom environments.

Teaching Practice Examiner — I serve as an examiner for teaching practice placements within the Faculty of Education, supervising and assessing student teachers on school placement and supporting their professional formation across computing and related subject areas.
